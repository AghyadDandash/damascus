Damascus is the capital of Syria
the city of Jasmine.

Damascus is considered one of the oldest cities in the world that has been ruled by many human civilizations. The name of the city of Jasmine is called the name of the city of Damascus; because of the spread of jasmine trees in its neighborhoods, and regions became the smell of jasmine part of its air, the name of Damascus is due It was known as Damaski, and in the Aramaic period its name was changed to Dimashqo. and the Arabic name of the city was derived from its Aramaic name since then known as Damascus.

The history of Damascus.

The history of Damascus Damascus was an important political center since 1100 BC, where it was the capital of the Kingdom of Aram, and then came under Greek rule, then Roman rule, and by 125 AD the city was a major urban center, and in 634 AD Damascus entered into its Arab-Islamic era, In the meantime, the Caliphs took control of the country and then came under the control of a number of powers over the next 1,000 years, and by the 16th century the Ottoman Empire intervened to prevent the alliance between the Mamluks and Persians. Economically important, and in the years ahead The first night of the twentieth century saw an Arab uprising against the Turkish Empire, and remained under the control of the French until 1941, in that year Syria gained its independence, and Damascus remained the capital of the country.

Cultural and aesthetic landmarks in Damascus.

The Umayyad Mosque

The Umayyad Mosque in Damascus is one of the most famous cultural and religious landmarks in the city of Damascus. It dates back to the era of the Umayyad rule of Damascus. Featured.

Damascus markets

Damascus markets are a group of old commercial markets that exist in Damascus, and rely on a popular architectural style, and is characterized by the existence of ceilings covering these markets, which gives people freedom to move between them, and protect them from exposure to sunlight, rain in winter, and the most famous of these markets : Hamidiya market, and Bzorip market.

The Arab House of Damascus

The Arab House of Damascus is one of the most famous aesthetic landmarks in the city of Damascus, and is one of the foundations of the old city of Damascus. The interior design of the house is beautiful and beautiful

Damascene Theater

Damascus theater is one of the oldest theaters in Syria, and its foundation dates back to the nineteenth century, and worked on the establishment of Abu Khalil Qabbani, he gave Damascus theater many famous plays, and contributed to the spread of theatrical art throughout Damascus.
